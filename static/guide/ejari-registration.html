How to register Ejari
in Dubai — step by step

Updated June 2026 · ⏱ 10 min to complete online · Fee: AED 122.50

Ejari is Dubai's mandatory tenancy registration system. Every rental contract must be registered through it — without Ejari you cannot connect DEWA, renew a residency visa, or file a dispute. This guide walks you through the full process.

Option A — Online
2

Download Dubai REST and log in

Download the Dubai REST app (iOS or Android) or open the DLD website. Log in using your UAE Pass — the government digital ID app that verifies your Emirates ID.
No UAE Pass yet? Create one at uaepass.ae — you'll need your Emirates ID number and a selfie. Takes about 5 minutes.
3

Navigate to "Register Ejari"

Inside the app: tap Services → Real Estate Services → Ejari → Register Ejari. Enter your Emirates ID number when prompted.
4

Upload your documents

Upload photos or PDFs of each document. The system will extract details automatically. Check every field carefully before proceeding.
Tenancy contractMust use DLD standard format
Tenant Emirates IDFront side, valid and not expired
Landlord Emirates IDOr passport if non-UAE resident
Title DeedProperty address must match contract exactly
Previous Ejari certificateRenewals only
⚠️ The property address in your tenancy contract must exactly match the title deed. Even minor differences (e.g. "Apt" vs "Apartment") can cause rejection.
5

Pay the registration fee

Review all extracted details, then pay online. Accepted: credit/debit card, Apple Pay, Google Pay.
Online (Dubai REST / DLD website)AED 122.50 incl. VAT
Typing centre (walk-in)AED 215–220 incl. VAT
The fee is the same for new registration and renewal.

Is the application approved?

✓ Approved

✗ Rejected
Fix and resubmit
Read the rejection reason. Common issues: address mismatch, expired ID, wrong document format. Correct and resubmit — no additional fee for resubmission.
✓

Ejari certificate issued — you're done

Your Ejari certificate contains a unique registration number. You'll need it to activate DEWA, for visa renewals, and for any rental dispute filings.

→ File at the Rental Dispute Centre if you have a dispute
