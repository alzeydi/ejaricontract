How to cancel Ejari
registration in Dubai

Updated June 2026 · ⏱ 5 minutes online · Fee: free

When you move out of a Dubai rental, you should formally cancel your Ejari registration. Cancellation is free, takes about 5 minutes online via the Dubai REST app, and releases the property record so the landlord can register the next tenant.

◆

What's your situation?

✓ Contract ended
Standard cancellation
You can submit cancellation alone, no landlord signature needed.
✗ Early termination
Landlord must e-sign
Both parties confirm via UAE Pass. Disputes go to the RDC.
1

Close your DEWA account first

Submit DEWA Move Out via the DEWA app, pay the final bill, and download the DEWA clearance confirmation. The Dubai REST app asks for this proof during Ejari cancellation.
See our DEWA Move Out / Transfer guide if you're relocating, or close the account if you're leaving Dubai.
2

Open Dubai REST app

Log in with UAE Pass, go to Services → Ejari → Cancel Contract. Select the active Ejari registration you want to cancel from the list. 📱 Dubai REST recommended
3

Upload supporting documents

Upload:
  • Existing Ejari certificate
  • DEWA clearance / final bill receipt
  • Property handover form (if your landlord requires it)
Select cancellation reason: End of contract, Early termination, Sale of property, or Other.
4

Submit and (if needed) await landlord signature

For end-of-contract cancellations, the system processes immediately. For early termination, the landlord receives a notification and must e-sign within Dubai REST — usually within 24–48 hours.
⚠️ If the landlord refuses to sign an early-termination cancellation, file at the Rental Dispute Centre — they can issue a binding cancellation order.
✓

Cancellation confirmed — Ejari closed

Confirmation email arrives within minutes. The Ejari record is closed in DLD systems, freeing the property for the next tenant. 🆓 No cancellation fee
i

Getting your security deposit back

Ejari cancellation does not handle the rental deposit — that is between you and the landlord. Document the property's condition with photos and a signed handover report. If the landlord refuses to return the deposit, file at the Rental Dispute Centre — most deposit disputes resolve within 15 days.
