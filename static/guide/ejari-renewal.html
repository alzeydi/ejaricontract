How to renew Ejari
in Dubai — step by step

Updated April 2026 · ⏱ 10 min online · Fee: AED 122.50

Ejari registration must be renewed every time your tenancy contract is renewed — even if you stay with the same landlord at the same address. The renewal process is faster than the initial registration because the system already has your property on file. Here's how to do it online in under 10 minutes.

◆

Is this a renewal or a new contract?

Renewal applies only when you stay with the same landlord at the same property. Anything else counts as a new registration.
✓ Renewal
Same landlord, same address
Continue with this guide.
✗ New contract
New landlord or new property
Even within the same building. Use the new registration guide →
1

Sign the renewed tenancy contract

Before you can renew Ejari, both parties must sign a renewed tenancy contract in the official DLD format with new dates. The agreed annual rent must comply with the RERA rent calculator — increases above the permitted percentage are not enforceable and can be challenged at the Rental Dispute Centre.
💡 Need a fresh DLD-format contract? Ejari Helper auto-fills it from your previous Ejari and Emirates IDs in seconds.
2

Gather the required documents

Renewal needs almost the same paperwork as new registration, plus your previous Ejari certificate.
Previous Ejari certificatePDF or printed copy
Renewed tenancy contractDLD format, signed by both parties
Tenant Emirates IDValid, not expired
Landlord Emirates IDOr passport for non-resident landlords
Title DeedSame as previous registration
⚠️ If your Emirates ID has been renewed since the last Ejari, upload the new one — the system cross-checks against the latest record.
3

Open Dubai REST and select Renew Ejari

Open the Dubai REST app (iOS or Android) or visit dubailand.gov.ae. Log in with UAE Pass, then go to Services → Real Estate Services → Ejari → Renew Ejari. 📱 Dubai REST app recommended
The app shows all your active Ejari certificates. Select the one being renewed — the system pre-fills the lessor, tenant, and property details from the previous record.
4

Upload the new contract and verify details

Upload the renewed contract PDF and check every pre-filled field. Pay attention to the contract dates, annual rent, and mode of payment — these change at renewal even when other fields don't.
If anything in the property section needs to change (e.g. updated DEWA Premises Number after a building renumbering), edit it now. After submission, only the dates and rent can be amended — property fields are locked.
5

Pay the renewal fee

Renewal costs the same as new registration — there is no discount for renewing. Pay online by card, Apple Pay or Google Pay.
Online via Dubai RESTAED 122.50 incl. VAT
Walk-in at typing centreAED 215–220 incl. VAT
◆

Is the renewal approved?

✓ Approved
New certificate issued instantly
Sent by email and available in Dubai REST. The Ejari registration number stays the same — only the validity period extends.
✗ Rejected
Most common cause
Mismatch between the new contract dates and the previous certificate's expiry. The new contract should start the day after the old one ends.
✓

Renewed Ejari issued — keep using your existing services

Your DEWA, residency visa, and any other services tied to Ejari continue without interruption. The Ejari number on your new certificate stays the same; only the validity dates change. ⚡ Issued same day
⚠️

What if your Ejari has already expired?

There is no fixed late-renewal penalty, but an expired Ejari blocks several things:
  • Visa renewals for tenant and family — GDRFA requires an active Ejari
  • RDC case filings — you cannot file at the Rental Dispute Centre without active registration
  • DEWA service changes — Move In, Move Out, name change all require valid Ejari
  • School / parking permits issued by Dubai municipality often require Ejari proof
If your Ejari is expired, sign the renewed contract first, then run the renewal process the same way — there is no separate "late renewal" path. The new certificate covers the new term going forward.
